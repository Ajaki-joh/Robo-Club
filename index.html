Weekly STEM Exploration

Discovery Saturdays

Every Saturday morning, curious minds aged 6–15 gather to explore, build, and discover. Discovery Saturdays is our flagship weekly program — a safe, structured space where children take their first real steps into coding, robotics, and technology through projects they actually care about finishing.

When
Every Saturday
1:00 AM – 3:30 PM
Age Range
6–18 years old
All skill levels
Format
Weekly sessions
Term-based intake

How a typical Saturday runs

01
Warm-Up Challenge A quick puzzle or mini-game that gets brains switched on and ideas flowing
02
Concept & Demo Instructors introduce the day's topic — coding, electronics, or robotics — with a live demo
03
Hands-On Build Students get their hands on kits and code — guided builds with room to experiment and ask questions
04
Share & Reflect Each student shows what they made, explains what worked, and gets feedback from the group

Skills Your Child Will Gain

  • Block-based coding with Scratch — games, animations, stories
  • Introduction to robotics — motors, sensors, and basic circuits
  • Computational thinking and structured problem-solving
  • Creative project design — from idea to working prototype
  • Teamwork, communication, and peer collaboration
  • Digital literacy and responsible technology use

Vacation Intensive

Builders Bootcamp

When school is out, the real building begins. Builders Bootcamp is our vacation intensive where students go all-in on robotics and AI — spending dedicated days designing, wiring, coding, and presenting complete projects from scratch. No theory without practice. Every session ends with something real.

When
School vacations

Format

Multi-day bootcamp
Who It's For
Ages 6–18

How the bootcamp runs

01
Brief & Design Students pick a robotics or AI challenge and sketch their solution
02
Build & Wire Hands-on construction — Arduino circuits, sensors, servo motors, and AI modules
03
Code & Test Bring the build to life with code — debug, iterate, and refine until it works
04
Demo Day Every student presents their finished project to peers, instructors, and parents

Projects Your Child Will Build

  • Line-following and obstacle-avoiding robots
  • Arduino smart home devices (auto lights, water monitors)
  • AI image and voice recognition demos
  • Autonomous vehicles and remote-controlled machines
  • Sensor-based environmental monitoring systems
  • Machine learning mini-projects with real datasets
